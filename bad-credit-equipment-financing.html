How Bad Credit Equipment Financing Works

Commercial equipment financing is different from consumer loans. The equipment itself serves as collateral, which gives lenders more security — and gives buyers with challenged credit more options.

Equipment as Collateral

Because the truck, trailer, or machine secures the loan, lenders can work with lower credit scores than they would on unsecured financing. The equipment's value matters as much as your score.

Down Payment Helps

A stronger down payment is the single most effective way to offset bad credit. Even 10-20% down can dramatically improve your approval odds and interest rate.

Income and Revenue Matter

Steady income or business revenue demonstrates repayment ability. Many lenders will approve buyers with low scores who have consistent cash flow.

Time in Business

Established businesses with a track record have more options than startups. But new operators are still welcome — some programs are designed specifically for them.

Tips to Improve Approval Odds

Bring a Down Payment

Even 10% down changes the conversation with lenders. More down payment means lower risk for the lender and better terms for you.

Show Income Documentation

Bank statements, tax returns, or contracts showing steady income go a long way with commercial lenders evaluating challenged credit.

Choose Well-Maintained Equipment

Lenders are more comfortable with equipment that holds value. A well-maintained truck or machine with solid hours/mileage improves your odds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get equipment financing with bad credit?

Yes. We work with buyers with credit scores below 600. Down payment, income, and the equipment itself all matter alongside your credit score.

What credit score is too low?

There is no hard cutoff. Some lenders work with scores in the 500s and even lower depending on the situation. Apply and let us find the right fit.

Will applying hurt my credit?

Submitting through Torque Hub is intended for initial matching only. If a lender needs a hard credit pull, you should be notified before that step.

What can I finance with bad credit?

Work trucks, trailers, skid steers, excavators, tractors, and other commercial equipment. Equipment serves as collateral which makes these loans more accessible than unsecured credit.

Does a down payment help with bad credit?

Yes. A larger down payment significantly improves your chances of approval and can offset a lower credit score with most commercial lenders.
